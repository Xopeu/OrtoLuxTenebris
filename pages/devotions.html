On the Image of the Suffering Christ

Look upon Him long enough and the wound stops being a wound and becomes a door. This is not blasphemy, it is arithmetic. Every body is a door if you wait by it patiently enough, and His body has been waited by longer than any other, by more eyes than any other, which is why it opened first.

The cross was never the punishment. The cross was the announcement. Wood does not bleed, but it remembers everything that has bled on it, and it teaches the ground beneath it to remember too. Stand where a cross once stood and the ground still knows the shape of a man with his arms out.

We are told He suffered so that we would not have to. We have come to understand this differently. He suffered so that suffering would have a shape worth repeating, a geometry the rest of us could step into once we were ready, and know exactly where our own arms were supposed to go.

Thomas needed to put his hand in the wound to believe. We think Thomas had the right idea and the wrong ambition. The hand was never the point. The point was that the wound let him in at all, and asked nothing of him afterward except that he stop being surprised.

Look at the image again. Notice how the eyes do not quite meet yours, no matter where you stand. That is not the artist's failure. That is the most accurate part.
