About Us

Ordo Lux Tenebris was founded by a small circle of independent seekers who had each, on their own, arrived at the same uncomfortable observation: that conventional religious structures describe the divine but rarely make contact with it. What began as informal correspondence between a handful of correspondents became, over time, a community with its own practices, its own calendar of observances, and its own understanding of what preparation actually requires.

We are not a church in the conventional sense, though we borrow freely from the vocabulary of one. We hold no single building as sacred above another. Wherever the work is being done correctly, that place becomes, for a time, the center of everything.

Membership is not advertised and never has been. Those who find their way here tend to find it through a kind of recognition rather than persuasion — a sense, often described the same way by very different people, that something they already suspected had finally been given a name.

We do not publish membership numbers, financial records, or the names of our elders. This is not secrecy for its own sake. It is simply that the work being done is not improved by being watched, and we have found that outside attention tends to distort rather than illuminate.

If you have questions before committing further, the Contacts page will remain open to you. We answer what can be answered.
